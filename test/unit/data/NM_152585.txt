            Summary: This gene encodes a protein containing an RNA-binding
            motif in the N-terminus and four SRGY (serine, arginine, glycine,
            tyrosine) boxes in the C-terminus. Multiple copies of this gene are
            found in the AZFb azoospermia factor region of chromosome Y and the
            encoded protein is thought to be involved in spermatogenesis. Most
            copies of this locus are pseudogenes, although six highly similar
            copies have full-length ORFs and are considered functional. Four
            functional copies of this gene are found within inverted repeat
            IR2; two functional copies of this gene are found in palindrome P3,
            along with two copies of PTPN13-like, Y-linked. [provided by
            RefSeq, Jul 2008].
